Writing Korean

Way to make it here! You ready to start learning Korean? The goal here is to teach you the basics of Korean writing in a couple hours. You won't be fluent but you will be able to read signs.

Introduction

Although Korean looks like a character system, it is actually an alphabet of 14 basic consonants and 10 basic vowels. The letters are stacked together in syllable blocks that make them look more complex.

Take, for example the word 'hangul' (Korean writing).

Practice

Syllable Blocks

With the basics under your belt, it's time to start putting them together in syllable blocks

Rule #1

Write the characters from top left to bottom right

Rule #2

Every syllable will have at least one consonant and vowel

Rule #3

A block always starts with a consonant

Rule #4

The vowel shape determines the the shape of the stacking

Hmmm...how does that work?

Vertical vowels are placed to the right of the consonant while horizontal vowels are placed under the consonant

If there is a consonant at the end of the syllable, you put it underneath

If there are two consonants at the end of the syllable, you put them both underneath

The pattern for complex vowels is similar. We'll learn these vowels later.
